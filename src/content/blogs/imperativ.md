# Der Imperativ: How to Give Commands in German

The **Imperativ** is the form you use when you tell someone to do something.

That sounds bossy, but it is not only for commands. German uses the imperative for many everyday situations:

* giving instructions
* making requests
* offering advice
* warning someone
* encouraging a friend

Examples:

* *Komm bitte hierher!*  
  Come here, please!

* *Öffnen Sie das Buch.*  
  Open the book.

* *Sei vorsichtig!*  
  Be careful!

The important part: German has different imperative forms depending on who you are talking to.

---

## 1. The Four Main Imperative Forms

German distinguishes between informal singular, informal plural, formal, and "let's".

| You are talking to... | Form | Example |
|-----------------------|------|---------|
| one friend | **du** form | Komm! |
| several friends | **ihr** form | Kommt! |
| formal person/group | **Sie** form | Kommen Sie! |
| yourself + others | **wir** form | Kommen wir! |

In real life, the most common forms are **du**, **ihr**, and **Sie**.

---

## 2. The Du Imperative

For the **du** imperative, start with the **du** form of the verb and remove **-st**.

| Infinitive | Du Form | Imperative |
|------------|---------|------------|
| kommen | du kommst | Komm! |
| machen | du machst | Mach! |
| lernen | du lernst | Lern! |
| trinken | du trinkst | Trink! |
| fragen | du fragst | Frag! |

Examples:

* *Komm bitte!*  
  Come, please!

* *Mach die Tür zu!*  
  Close the door!

* *Lern die Wörter!*  
  Learn the words!

You usually do **not** say **du** in the imperative.

* Wrong: *Du komm!*
* Correct: *Komm!*

---

## 3. The Optional -e Ending

Many verbs can add an optional **-e** in the du imperative.

| Short Form | With -e | Meaning |
|------------|---------|---------|
| Komm! | Komme! | Come! |
| Frag! | Frage! | Ask! |
| Lern! | Lerne! | Learn! |
| Mach! | Mache! | Do / make! |

In modern everyday German, the short form is more common:

* *Komm schnell!*
* *Frag deine Lehrerin!*
* *Lern jeden Tag zehn Minuten!*

The **-e** form can sound more formal, written, old-fashioned, or poetic depending on the verb and context.

---

## 4. Important Irregular Du Forms

Some verbs change their stem in the **du** imperative.

| Infinitive | Du Form | Imperative |
|------------|---------|------------|
| geben | du gibst | Gib! |
| nehmen | du nimmst | Nimm! |
| sprechen | du sprichst | Sprich! |
| lesen | du liest | Lies! |
| sehen | du siehst | Sieh! |
| essen | du isst | Iss! |

Examples:

* *Gib mir bitte das Buch.*  
  Give me the book, please.

* *Nimm den Bus!*  
  Take the bus!

* *Sprich langsam!*  
  Speak slowly!

* *Lies den Satz noch einmal!*  
  Read the sentence again!

The verb **sein** is very irregular:

* *Sei ruhig!*  
  Be quiet!

* *Sei vorsichtig!*  
  Be careful!

---

## 5. The Ihr Imperative

The **ihr** imperative is wonderfully simple. It is exactly the same as the normal **ihr** form, but without the pronoun.

| Infinitive | Ihr Form | Imperative |
|------------|----------|------------|
| kommen | ihr kommt | Kommt! |
| machen | ihr macht | Macht! |
| lernen | ihr lernt | Lernt! |
| lesen | ihr lest | Lest! |

Examples:

* *Kommt bitte rein!*  
  Come in, please!

* *Macht die Bücher auf!*  
  Open the books!

* *Lest den Text!*  
  Read the text!

You are speaking to a group of people you address informally.

---

## 6. The Formal Sie Imperative

For formal situations, use the infinitive plus **Sie**.

| Infinitive | Formal Imperative |
|------------|-------------------|
| kommen | Kommen Sie! |
| warten | Warten Sie! |
| sprechen | Sprechen Sie! |
| unterschreiben | Unterschreiben Sie! |

Examples:

* *Warten Sie bitte hier.*  
  Please wait here.

* *Sprechen Sie langsam.*  
  Speak slowly.

* *Füllen Sie das Formular aus.*  
  Fill out the form.

Unlike the **du** and **ihr** forms, you must include **Sie**.

---

## 7. The Wir Imperative: Let's...

The **wir** imperative means "let's".

Use the infinitive plus **wir**:

* *Gehen wir!*  
  Let's go!

* *Machen wir eine Pause.*  
  Let's take a break.

* *Wiederholen wir die Regel.*  
  Let's repeat the rule.

This form sounds a little structured, but it is useful in classrooms, meetings, and group situations.

---

## 8. Imperative with Separable Verbs

Separable verbs behave exactly as you would expect from German word order: the prefix goes to the end.

| Infinitive | Du | Ihr | Sie |
|------------|----|-----|-----|
| aufmachen | Mach auf! | Macht auf! | Machen Sie auf! |
| anrufen | Ruf an! | Ruft an! | Rufen Sie an! |
| zuhören | Hör zu! | Hört zu! | Hören Sie zu! |
| mitkommen | Komm mit! | Kommt mit! | Kommen Sie mit! |

Examples:

* *Mach bitte das Fenster auf!*  
  Please open the window!

* *Ruf mich später an!*  
  Call me later!

* *Hören Sie gut zu!*  
  Listen carefully!

If you already know separable verbs, the imperative follows the same main-clause logic.

---

## 9. Imperative with Reflexive Verbs

With reflexive verbs, keep the reflexive pronoun.

| Infinitive | Du | Ihr | Sie |
|------------|----|-----|-----|
| sich setzen | Setz dich! | Setzt euch! | Setzen Sie sich! |
| sich beeilen | Beeil dich! | Beeilt euch! | Beeilen Sie sich! |
| sich entspannen | Entspann dich! | Entspannt euch! | Entspannen Sie sich! |

Examples:

* *Setz dich bitte.*  
  Please sit down.

* *Beeilt euch!*  
  Hurry up!

* *Entspannen Sie sich.*  
  Relax.

This is where knowing reflexive pronouns helps a lot.

---

## 10. How to Sound Polite

The imperative can sound direct. To make it softer, add polite words.

Useful softeners:

* **bitte** — please
* **doch** — softens or encourages
* **mal** — makes it more casual
* **bitte mal** — please, casually

Compare:

| Direct | Softer |
|--------|--------|
| Komm her! | Komm bitte her. |
| Warte! | Warte mal. |
| Setz dich! | Setz dich doch. |
| Öffnen Sie das Fenster! | Öffnen Sie bitte das Fenster. |

German can be direct, but **bitte**, **doch**, and **mal** make a big difference in everyday speech.

---

## 11. Negative Imperatives

To tell someone not to do something, use **nicht**.

* *Komm nicht zu spät!*  
  Do not come late!

* *Mach das nicht!*  
  Do not do that!

* *Rauchen Sie hier bitte nicht.*  
  Please do not smoke here.

With nouns, use **kein** when the meaning is "no / not any".

* *Mach keine Fehler!*  
  Do not make mistakes!

* *Hab keine Angst!*  
  Do not be afraid!

This is a nice place where the difference between **nicht** and **kein** becomes practical.

---

## Summary Checklist

* **Du imperative:** remove **-st** from the du form: *du kommst -> Komm!*
* **Ihr imperative:** use the normal ihr form without **ihr**: *Kommt!*
* **Sie imperative:** infinitive + **Sie**: *Kommen Sie!*
* **Wir imperative:** infinitive + **wir**: *Gehen wir!*
* Separable prefixes go to the end: *Mach das Licht aus!*
* Reflexive pronouns stay: *Setz dich!*
* Add **bitte**, **doch**, or **mal** to sound friendlier.
